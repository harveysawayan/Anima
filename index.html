CURATOR’S NOTE

The Living Archive

ANIMA is a curated exploration of life through art where instinct, form, and emotion converge. Each piece reflects a different perspective of the animal world, capturing its quiet intensity and raw presence. Across physical and digital mediums, this collection transforms observation into expression, allowing nature to be seen not as distant but as something deeply felt.

THE ARCHIVE

THE ANIMA PROJECT

Why ANIMA?
The name ANIMA was chosen because it literally means "breath" or "soul" in Latin. In this gallery, it’s about capturing the spirit of the animals themselves. It is not just a flat picture of them but the feeling of them being alive. The goal was to build a site that feels as raw and intense as the nature it shows, moving away from a basic website and making it feel more like you're walking through a high-end physical exhibit dedicated to the animal world.

How it was built
This site wasn't made with a website builder where you can just drag and drop things. It was built manually using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript inside VS Code. This process is often called "vibe coding"—basically, instead of following a strict plan, I just kept changing the code until the "feeling" or the "vibe" of the page looked right.

The Reality of Design
The project is made up of almost 2,000 lines of code. Designing it was actually pretty frustrating because, while you’re working, you don’t see the art or the colors. All you see are thousands of lines of text, random numbers, and symbols. You have to hope that the math and the tags you are typing actually turn into a good-looking website when you finally hit refresh. It’s a lot of trial and error to get the symbols on the screen to look like the vision in your head.
